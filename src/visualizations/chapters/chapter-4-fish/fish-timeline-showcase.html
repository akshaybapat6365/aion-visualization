The Fish Symbol Through Time

Understanding Jung's Aion Through the Evolution of a Symbol

In "Aion," Carl Jung traces how the fish symbol transformed from an ancient fertility symbol to the Christian Ichthys, and how this relates to the astrological ages. This visualization will guide you through 2,500 years of symbolic evolution, showing how collective human consciousness shifts with the precession of the equinoxes.

The Aeon of the Fish

Pre-Christian Era

The Ancient Fish

500 BCE - 0 CE

Before Christianity, the fish was already a powerful symbol. In ancient cultures, it represented fertility, the feminine principle, and the unconscious depths.

The astrological Age of Pisces was beginning. Two fish swimming in opposite directions would come to represent the fundamental duality of human nature.

"The fish is a content of the unconscious, for it lives in the water, which is the commonest symbol for the unconscious." - C.G. Jung, Aion

The Dual Fish

313 - 1500 CE

As Christianity established itself, the fish symbol evolved. The two fish of Pisces came to represent Christ and Antichrist, good and evil, the conscious and unconscious.

Jung observed how medieval alchemists used the fish to symbolize the prima materia - the raw, unconscious material that must be transformed through the opus.

"The one fish which is Christ is followed by the Antichrist... This is the dilemma of the opposites inherent in the archetype." - C.G. Jung, Aion

The Fading Fish

1500 - 2000 CE

As we approach the end of the Age of Pisces, the fish symbol's power wanes. The rise of science and rationalism represents a shift in collective consciousness.

Jung saw this as the necessary integration of the shadow - the dark fish must be acknowledged alongside the light. The opposites must be united.

"We are living in what the Greeks called the Kairos - the right moment - for a 'metamorphosis of the gods.'" - C.G. Jung, Aion

The Water Bearer

2000 - 4000 CE

We stand at the threshold of the Age of Aquarius. The Water Bearer pours out the waters of consciousness, symbolizing a new level of human awareness.

Jung believed this transition would bring profound psychological changes - a new relationship between conscious and unconscious, individual and collective.

"The approach of the Aquarian age will constellate the problem of the union of opposites. It will then no longer be possible to write off evil as the mere privation of good." - C.G. Jung, Aion
