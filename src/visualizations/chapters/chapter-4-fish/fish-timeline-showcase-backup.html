Understanding the Fish Symbol Through Time

Ancient Origins

Before Christianity, fish symbols appeared in various cultures as representations of fertility, abundance, and the mysterious depths of water - the primordial element of life and the unconscious.

Christian Ichthys

The Greek word ΙΧΘΥΣ (Ichthys) became an acronym for "Jesus Christ, Son of God, Savior." During Roman persecution, Christians used the fish symbol as a secret sign of recognition.

Astrological Significance

The Age of Pisces, beginning around the time of Christ, is symbolized by two fish swimming in opposite directions - representing the fundamental duality of the Christian aeon.

Alchemical Fish

In alchemy, the fish represents the prima materia swimming in the waters of the unconscious, undergoing transformation in the vessel of the psyche.

Psychological Meaning

Jung saw fish as symbols of contents rising from the unconscious. Dreams of fish often indicate contact with deeper layers of the psyche and the process of individuation.

Aquarian Transition

As we approach the Age of Aquarius, the symbol shifts from fish (unconscious immersion) to the water-bearer (conscious handling of psychic contents).
